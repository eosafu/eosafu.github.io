Biography

I am a trained statistician specialized in the development of advanced spatial statistical and AI methods for analyzing public health and biomedical data to uncover spatial patterns of diseases and health conditions at population and molecular scales. I earned my Ph.D. from the University of São Paulo, Brazil. I am a Postdoctoral fellow at the Biostatistics and Computational Biology Branch of the National Institute of Environmental Health Sciences, NC. My methods have been applied to map the prevalence of diseases such as malaria, anemia, and airborne infections across diverse geographical settings at a population scale, and have been utilized to map the impact of therapeutic interventions on cellular neighborhoods in a tumor microenvironment at a molecular scale.

Research Interest

Statistics, Biostatistics, and Deep Learning, applied to study diseases and health conditions.
